QUESTIONS

What landlords ask us most

How much does property management cost?

Management fees across our Sydney, Brisbane and Melbourne portfolio typically sit between 7 and 9 percent of rent collected, plus letting and ancillary fees. We quote a single, all-inclusive rate with your rental appraisal so there are no surprises on your statement.

How often can rent be increased?

In NSW, QLD and VIC, rent can be increased no more than once every 12 months. Notice requirements vary by state: 60 days in NSW and VIC, two months in QLD. We benchmark your rent against live suburb data at every renewal so each review is timed and evidenced properly.

How quickly can you lease my property?

Leasing time depends on presentation, pricing and season. Across our portfolio, well-presented and correctly priced properties typically lease within around two weeks of listing. Your appraisal includes a realistic timeframe for your specific property.

What does a rental appraisal include?

A recommended rent range backed by current comparable rentals in your suburb, local demand data, and practical presentation recommendations to maximise your return. It is free and there is no obligation to proceed.
